<strong>How to centralize all student information in a single system</strong>

<br>

<strong>Angle: teacher/tutor view</strong>

<br>

Unified pedagogical monitoring. Governance in <a href="/en/blog/school-document-management-digital-records-2026">digital file 2026</a>.

The digital academic record and the management of students at school scattered in folders, spreadsheets and emails generate errors, delays and frustration. Centralizing all student information in a single system allows the secretary, teachers and management to access updated data, with traceability and regulatory compliance. This article explains how to implement a digital academic record and improve student management at school.

<br>

<strong>What does a digital academic record include</strong>

<br>

Personal data, academic history, attendance, grades, observations, documentation (authorizations, reports) and communications with family. A well-structured digital transcript centralizes all of this in one place. Managing students at school with digital records eliminates searching in folders and reduces transcription errors.

<br>

<strong>Benefits of the digital academic record</strong>

<br>

<strong>1. Immediate access</strong>

<br>

Secretary, teachers and management consult student information without searching multiple sites. Student management in a digital school saves time in each consultation. The digital academic record is always available to anyone with permissions.

<br>

<strong>2. Traceability and compliance</strong>

<br>

Record of who has accessed, what has been modified and when. The digital academic record makes it easier to comply with GDPR and respond to inspections. The management of students in schools with an audit provides legal security.

<br>

<strong>3. Integrated communication</strong>

<br>

Communications with the family are recorded in the digital academic record. Student management at school that integrates messages avoids losing the thread of conversations or agreements.

<br>

<strong>4. Automatic reports</strong>

<br>

Generate reports by student or group for tutors, orientation or inspection. The digital academic record with integrated reports saves hours of manual work. Managing students at school with analytics gives visibility.

<br>

<strong>How to migrate to the digital academic record</strong>

<br>

Defines what data is critical (registration, history, documentation) and what format it is in. Plan the migration in phases: first current data, then historical data if applicable. Student management in a digital school requires data validation and team training. An experienced digital transcript provider can guide you through the process.

<br>

<strong>Digitize the entire historical archive?</strong>

<br>

Prioritizes current course and active documents; the history by phases.

<br>

<strong>What the digital file should contain</strong>

<br>

- **Identifying data:** Student, tutors, course and contacts updated in ERP.
- **Academic history:** Grades, continuous evaluation and observations by course.
- **Authorizations:** Image, school outings and collection with digital signature.
- **Health reports:** Allergies, medication and protocols accessible by nursing.
- **Incidents:** Coexistence and tutoring monitoring with chronological record.
- **NEAE Documents:** Adaptations and agreements with access restricted by role.
- **Archived communications:** Institutional messages linked to the file.

Each access registered by user and date.

<br>

<strong>Typical roles</strong>

<br>

- **Administration:** Contracts, invoices, direct debits and registrations or cancellations.
- **Tutoring:** Academic monitoring, group incidents and travel authorizations.
- **Nursing:** Allergies, medication and health protocols.
- **Address:** Minutes, internal protocols and closing reports.
- **Family:** Consultation of your documents and payment status.

<br>

<strong>Measurable benefit</strong>

<br>

Locate a document: from 12 minutes in a physical folder to seconds with search. On inspection, response the same day.

<br>

<strong>Application in your center</strong><br>

In practice, the center's treasury improves when billing, collections and reconciliation share the same data source. Avoid exporting to Excel to reconcile: each export is an opportunity for error and delay in responding to families.

<br>

<strong>Context in Spain: student records and documentation</strong>

<br>

Image authorizations, allergies, NEAE, collection authorizations and service contracts must be accessible by role (guardian, nursing, administration) with access registration. The LOPDGDD requires special diligence with minors: it is not enough to digitize PDFs in a shared folder without permissions or traceability.

A well-designed digital file avoids folders at reception, speeds up responses to families and inspection, and reduces the risk of health data or sensitive situations circulating by email without control. Each role should see only what is necessary (least privilege), with deletion blocking on critical documents and a record of who accessed it and when.

Consult with counsel the retention periods by type (academic, labor, tax). The system must allow deletion blocking and audited export in the event of a complaint or inspection. Digitizing the entire historical file in the first month is usually a mistake: prioritize current course and active documents, and migrate the history in phases.

<br>

<strong>Case study (Spain)</strong>

<br>

A private school digitized authorizations and incident reports. Average time to locate a student's document: from 12 minutes (physical folder) to 20 seconds (file search). They responded to a documentary inspection on the same day.
