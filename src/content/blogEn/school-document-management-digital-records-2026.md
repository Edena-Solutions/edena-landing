<strong>Document management in schools: goodbye to paper and lost files</strong>

<br>

A lost file is no small inconvenience: it involves legal risk, committee delays, complaints, and dozens of secretarial hours tracking down "who had the latest mail." In 2026, document management in schools, nurseries and academies is not about scanning and saving: it requires clear permissions, reading traces where appropriate, visibility by course, group or student, a defined deregistration or archiving cycle, and alignment with the GDPR. This last part (legal basis, retention, access, destruction) is defined by your DPO or advisor; The software facilitates structure and access controls, it does not replace regulatory criteria. Edena includes, in the base platform, the digital file and organizational chart of the center. With the Cloud module, add cloud documentation, folders, and visibility by group or student, as described in the public module catalog. No need to invent functionalities: the value is in connecting files, families, billing and collection in a single data criterion, without WeTransfer boxes or "v4_final_definitiva" versions in the email.

<br>

<strong>Why paper holds and loose attachment reinforces it</strong>

<br>

The paper passes from hand to hand, deteriorates and does not inform who consulted it. Email with attachment multiplies versions without history: if the family delivers the same document three times through three different channels, the secretariat ends up with three copies, no one knows which one is the good one, and the file never closes. A solid model links each document to the student's file, records who uploaded it, who can read it, when it was validated and what happens when the student changes stage, transfer or withdrawal. With that thread, the committee can answer in three seconds where the signed consent or the medical allergy certificate is, without searching through three trays and two cabinets.

<br>

<strong>What a documentary model should cover in an educational center</strong>

<br>

The minimum scheme includes coherent categories: admission (form, contract, identity), health (authorizations, allergies, special needs), academic (notes, evaluations, stage reports), communications (signed circulars, agreements with families) and incidents (record of facts, measures, notifications). Each category needs a person responsible, a retention period and criteria for who sees it. The family should access only their documents; the guardians, to what is necessary to exercise their function; direction and administration, according to their role, with traces of openness in the most sensitive ones.

<br>

<strong>GDPR applied to the file: what software is not</strong>

<br>

The platform provides tools: role access, audit trail, encryption, folders with controlled visibility. But the legal basis of the treatment, the retention period defined by law or center policy, the response procedure to access or deletion rights, and the impact assessment if applicable: that is determined by your legal team or DPO. Requiring that a supplier "guarantee GDPR" without that internal chain is confusing product with advice. Edena provides product criteria, not legal advice.

<strong>Honest implementation plan</strong><br>

Week 1: inventory of types of documents, those responsible, retention periods, who can access what and how the deletion or transfer is managed. Week 2: pilot load with a course or group, validation of quality criteria (readability, metadata, category). Week 3: team training, communication to families about the portal and guided loading. Week 4: extension and review. Accepting a hybrid plan is honest where regulation or practice still requires a physical signature or paper document, with a committed closing date for that exception.

<strong>Errors that are most repeated</strong>

<br>

- Network shared folder with no rules, no versioning, no locking - just another bigger drawer.
- Document photography on a mobile phone with poor quality, without metadata, without category criteria.
- Everyone sends and receives via WhatsApp, without registration or retention.
- Lack of training: the tool lies if no one feeds it in the correct phase.
- Measure "uploaded documents" without verifying quality, incident closure or risk reduction.

<br>

<strong>Impact on peaks: September, admission, inspections</strong>

<br>

September is the month where the document load skyrockets: registrations, contracts, authorizations, medical certificates, AMPA forms, ID photos. An orderly system absorbs that peak with progressive loading in previous phases, instead of collapsing the secretariat on the first day. In admission, the CRM with statuses and phases prevents a file from being left in limbo due to lack of annex, with an alert on the platform for the family and the team. In inspections, traceability erases the "should be somewhere" and allows you to show exactly what was communicated, when and to whom.

<strong>Document types by role</strong>

<br>

- **Administration:** Contracts, invoices, direct debits, registrations and cancellations.
- **Tutoring:** Academic monitoring, travel authorizations and incidents.
- **Nursing:** Allergies, medication and health protocols.
- **Address:** Minutes, internal protocols and closing reports.
- **Principle:** Each role sees only what is necessary (minimum privilege) with access logging.

<br>

<strong>Legal retention</strong>

<br>

- **Consult with advice** deadlines by type: academic, work, tax.
- **Deletion blocking** on documents subject to mandatory conservation.
- **Access record** before inspection, complaint or internal audit.
- **Migration by phases:** Prioritizes current course; The paper history can be digitized later.

<br>

<strong>Context in Spain: student records and documentation</strong>

<br>

Image authorizations, allergies, NEAE, collection authorizations and service contracts must be accessible by role (guardian, nursing, administration) with access registration. The LOPDGDD requires special diligence with minors: it is not enough to digitize PDFs in a shared folder without permissions or traceability.

A well-designed digital file avoids folders at reception, speeds up responses to families and inspection, and reduces the risk of health data or sensitive situations circulating by email without control. Each role should see only what is necessary (least privilege), with deletion blocking on critical documents and a record of who accessed it and when.

Consult with counsel the retention periods by type (academic, labor, tax). The system must allow deletion blocking and audited export in the event of a complaint or inspection. Digitizing the entire historical file in the first month is usually a mistake: prioritize current course and active documents, and migrate the history in phases.
