<strong>School ERP: the complete guide to digitize the management of your educational center</strong>

<br>

<strong>Angle: what is an ERP and its modules</strong>

<br>

File, collections, communication, admission. Difference with SIS in <a href="/en/blog/student-information-system-2026">SIS vs ERP</a>.

A school ERP integrates the administrative, financial and academic management of an educational center into a single platform. Billing, collections, records, attendance, communication with families and human resources can coexist in the same management software for schools, eliminating duplicates and automating processes. This guide explains what a school ERP is, what modules to include, and how to choose the right solution.

<br>

<strong>What is a school ERP and how it differs from an SIS</strong>

<br>

A school ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning) is a comprehensive system that manages the center's resources: financial, human, academic and operational. A SIS focuses on student information; A school ERP goes further and includes billing, payroll, inventory, cafeteria, routing and other processes. The most complete management software for schools is usually an ERP that integrates student, family and administration modules.

<br>

<strong>Key modules of a school ERP</strong>

<br>

<strong>1. Academic management and records</strong>

<br>

Enrollments, digital records, attendance, grades and evaluation. The school ERP centralizes academic information and connects it with the rest of the processes.

<br>

<strong>2. Billing and collection</strong>

<br>

Invoice generation, automatic reminders, online payments and integration with Verifactu. Good management software for schools automates billing and reduces non-payments.

<br>

<strong>3. Communication with families</strong>

<br>

App or portal for families, notifications, circulars and segmented messages. The school ERP unifies communication channels and avoids the chaos of emails and WhatsApp groups.

<br>

<strong>4. Human resources (optional)</strong>

<br>

Contracts, payroll, absences and staff training. Not all school ERPs include this module; Consider if you need it or if you use an external system.

<br>

<strong>5. Complementary services</strong>

<br>

Dining room, routes, extracurricular activities. School management software that covers these services simplifies billing and logistics.

<br>

<strong>How to choose a school ERP</strong>

<br>

Define the processes that generate the most time and complaints today. Check that the school ERP covers them natively, without depending on fragile integrations. Rate usability, security (GDPR, EU hosting) and support. Ask for references from centers of similar size and profile. Management software for schools that is not adopted or that requires constant training does not add value.

<br>

<strong>Common errors when implementing a school ERP</strong>

<br>

- Choose by price without evaluating total cost (training, migration, integrations).
- Do not involve the secretariat and administration in the decision.
- Assume that "anything goes" in data protection.
- Not planning the migration or the period of coexistence with the previous system.

<br>

<strong>Essential modules of a school ERP</strong>

<br>

- **Student-family file:** Master data, tutors and centralized documentation.
- **Billing and collections:** Recurring receipts, late payments and bank reconciliation.
- **Communication/family app:** Circulars, authorizations and incidents with traceability.
- **Admission and CRM:** Pipeline, post-visit monitoring and place reservation.
- **Documents with permissions:** Minimum privilege per role and access record.
- **Roles and audit:** Traceability before inspection and LOPDGDD.

Optional according to profile: transportation, dining room, library.

<br>

<strong>ERP vs loose tools</strong>

<br>

Three disconnected apps multiply transcription errors. The hidden cost is in secretarial hours, not just licenses.

<br>

<strong>Recommended implementation order</strong><br>

- **Master data:** Clean students, families, rates and courses before activating modules.
- **Collection:** First actual cycle of receipts; It is usually the main secretarial pain.
- **Communication:** Family portal when collections and files share source.
- **Admission:** CRM connected to registration and first payment.
- **Analytics:** Monthly Dashboard when operational data is reliable.

Skipping charges when cash flow is the main pain usually fails.

<br>

<strong>Context in Spain: what a school ERP requires in 2026</strong>

<br>

Beyond grades and attendance, school management software in Spain must cover billing (including Verifactu preparation), family portal or app, file with minor data under GDPR and LOPDGDD, and communications traceability. Buying an isolated SIS when the real pain is in secretarial, collections and families usually ends in duplicating data between three tools.

In nursery schools they weigh places, ratios, menus and allergies; in academies, bonuses, seasons and multiple venues; in private schools, differentiated fees, internal scholarships and commercial recruitment. An ERP that works in language academies may fall short if you manage child, NEAE or dining room ratios with billing per day.

Evaluates three-year TCO: license, migration, training, internal coordination hours and cost of bridge integrations. Request a demo with your real data (five families, one cycle of receipts) before deciding. Software that seems cheaper at the beginning can become more expensive if it forces you to add CRM, billing or communication separately.

<br>

<strong>Case study (Spain)</strong>

<br>

An 85-place nursery school in Girona evaluated three providers with the same checklist: enrollment, menus, billing and family app. The 10-week pilot with real data revealed that only one closed the application-enrollment-payment cycle without Excel. The change cost them 40 hours of migration, but they eliminated duplicate data in three tools.
