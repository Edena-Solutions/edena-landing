<strong>How to choose software for a nursery or daycare centre in 2026</strong>

<br>

A nursery is not a small school: the day-to-day is meals, naps, nappies, photos and families who want to know everything the moment it happens. That is why choosing software with school-sized criteria usually ends badly: enormous platforms the team never uses, or friendly diary apps that cannot invoice. This guide explains what nursery or daycare software really needs to cover, what to ask in every demo, and which warning signs to spot before you sign anything.

<br>

<strong>The problem: a dozen tools for a small team</strong>

<br>

The typical situation in a nursery: the daily diary is kept in paper notebooks, photos go out over WhatsApp, receipts come from a separate billing programme, children's records live in a spreadsheet and newsletters are printed. Each tool works, but none of them talk to each other, and the team — almost always small — loses hours every day copying data from one place to another. The cost is not just time: it is billing errors, photos travelling through insecure channels and families finding out about important things too late.

<br>

<strong>The four pillars it must cover</strong>

<br>

- <strong>Digital daily diary:</strong> log meals, naps and incidents in seconds, from the classroom, with the family seeing it instantly on their phone.
- <strong>Family communication:</strong> secure messaging, announcements with read receipts and photos shared only with authorised guardians.
- <strong>Billing and payments:</strong> fees, lunches and extra hours invoiced automatically every month, with SEPA direct debits and arrears tracking.
- <strong>Child records:</strong> a complete file for each child with allergies, health data, authorisations and contacts, available to the team at a glance.

If the software you are evaluating covers only one or two pillars, it does not eliminate the tool chaos: it reorganises it. The real difference is a single record per child feeding the diary, the invoice and the communication at once, the way purpose-built <a href="/en/nurseries">nursery and daycare management software</a> does.

<br>

<strong>The digital diary: the heart of the day</strong>

<br>

In the 0–3 age range, daily communication is not an extra: it is the product. Families drop off children who cannot tell them about their day, so the digital diary is their window into the classroom. Scrutinise how the day gets logged: if recording lunch for twenty children takes twenty screens, the team will be back on paper within a week. Look for group logging in a few taps, secure photos and instant visibility for the family. And check that the same information is stored in the child's record, not in an ephemeral feed.

<br>

<strong>Billing: where the month is won or lost</strong>

<br>

A nursery's finances run on monthly fees, extra hours, lunches and passes, with enrolments starting and ending all year round. The software must invoice all of it automatically, run the direct debits and flag unpaid receipts without anyone combing through lists by hand. In Spain, ask about Verifactu too: verifiable invoicing becomes mandatory for invoice issuers during 2027, and switching billing systems mid-year is the last thing you want. Better a provider that already complies today.

<br>

<strong>The questions to ask in every demo</strong>

<br>

- How long does it take an educator to log a full day for their group?
- Are photos shared only with each child's authorised families?
- Does billing run itself every month, with direct debits and arrears tracking?
- Who handles data migration and family onboarding?
- Is pricing per child, with no lock-in, and what exactly does it include?
- Is there a GDPR data-processing agreement, with data hosted in the EU?

<br>

<strong>Warning signs</strong>

<br>

Distrust prices that are not published and require "requesting a quote", multi-year lock-ins, family apps charged separately, and any provider who solves photos "over WhatsApp for now". Watch out for the opposite extreme too: big-school platforms full of secondary-education modules your team of five will never open. The right software for a nursery is the one whose demo talks about naps and nappies, not exam weightings.
